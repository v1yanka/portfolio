    Projects


    Data Science


    In this project, I explored how analysing hand-drawn spirals could help create a low-cost, non-invasive way to screen for Parkinson’s disease. The idea was to use features from these drawings - like how steady the pen was, how much pressure was applied, and the smoothness of movement - to distinguish between healthy individuals and people with Parkinson’s.


    Data Science


    In this project, I developed and trained a machine learning model to classify cherries, strawberries, and tomatoes - three visually similar fruits. I started with a simple Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) and then developed a complex Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). My final model reached an accuracy of 81% on unseen test data.

    Data Science


    I conducted an analysis of insurance claim data to develop a predictive model for claim occurrences. Using Python, I built and compared eight models, evaluating them with metrics like ROC AUC, accuracy, and precision. This project showcases my skills in data analysis, machine learning, and extracting actionable insights from complex datasets.
